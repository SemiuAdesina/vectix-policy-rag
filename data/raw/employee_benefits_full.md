## 1. Overview and Eligibility

The Global Health Plan is the primary medical, dental, vision, and mental health benefits program for all full-time VectixLogic employees. The company covers 100% of the employee premium and 50% for up to two dependents. Eligibility begins on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment. **Covered locations:** Nigeria (Lagos, Ibadan, and other states), United States (Wyoming and remote US-based staff).

---

## 2. Medical, Dental, Vision, and Mental Health

- **Inpatient/Outpatient:** Covered at network facilities; pre-authorization required for non-emergency admissions. Emergency care covered worldwide with 48-hour notification for non-network.
- **Dental:** Preventive at 100% (two cleanings/year); restorative 80%; major 50%. Annual maximum $1,500 per person.
- **Vision:** One exam/year at 100%; allowance $150 (adult) / $100 (dependents under 18) for lenses/frames or contacts.
- **Mental Health:** Parity with medical; up to 20 outpatient sessions/year without prior auth; EAP available and does not count toward limit.
- **Pharmacy:** Tier 1–3 copays; network and mail-order options; Nigeria network includes designated pharmacies in Lagos and Ibadan.

---

## 3. Claim Process Summary

- **Nigeria (network):** Present GHP ID card; provider submits claims. Reimbursement claims within 90 days with itemized receipts.
- **US (in-network):** Use carrier ID; out-of-network submit CMS-1500/UB-04 within 12 months. Coordination of benefits applies if other coverage exists.
- **Appeals:** 180 days from denial; submit in writing with supporting documentation.

## 5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Eligibility and Enrollment

**Q: When does my Global Health Plan coverage start?**  
A: Coverage begins on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment. For example, if you start on January 15, coverage is effective February 1.

**Q: Can I add my spouse and children?**  
A: Yes. The company pays 50% of the premium for up to two dependents (e.g., spouse and one child, or two children). Additional dependents can be added at your cost; rates are in the benefits portal.

**Q: I am a contractor or part-time. Am I eligible?**  
A: The Global Health Plan is for full-time employees only. Contractors and part-time staff should refer to their contract for any separate arrangements.

**Q: How do I enroll or change my elections?**  
A: New hires receive enrollment materials during onboarding. Changes are made during open enrollment (annually) or within 31 days of a qualifying life event (e.g., marriage, birth, loss of other coverage).

### Nigeria vs US Coverage

**Q: I work in Lagos but will be in the US for a month. Which network do I use?**  
A: Your network is based on your official work location on file with HR. If you are temporarily in the US, emergency care is covered worldwide; for non-emergency care in the US, contact HR or the plan administrator to confirm if you can use the US network or must pay and submit for reimbursement.

**Q: Can I use UCH if I am based in Lagos?**  
A: Yes. UCH (Ibadan) is in-network for all Nigeria-based employees. You may use any in-network facility regardless of your home office.

**Q: Where do I find the list of US in-network doctors?**  
A: US-based employees use the carrier’s online directory or mobile app (as provided in your enrollment packet). The list of Nigerian HMO providers is in Section 4 of this document and in the benefits portal.

### Claims and Reimbursement

**Q: How long do I have to submit a claim?**  
A: Nigeria: 90 days from the date of service. US: typically 12 months from the date of service (confirm in your SPD). Late submissions may be denied.

**Q: My claim was denied. What do I do?**  
A: You will receive a written explanation. You may appeal in writing within 180 days of the denial date, with any supporting documentation (e.g., physician letter, medical records). Send to the address on the denial notice; for Nigeria, contact HR for the correct appeals address.

**Q: I paid out of pocket at a non-network provider. Will I be reimbursed?**  
A: Reimbursement is at the plan’s “reasonable and customary” rates for the region. Amounts above that are your responsibility. Submit itemized receipts and the completed claim form within 90 days (Nigeria) or per SPD (US).

### Dental and Vision

**Q: How many dental cleanings are covered per year?**  
A: Two cleanings per calendar year at 100% when using a network dentist. X-rays and exams in connection with those cleanings are also covered.

**Q: What is the vision allowance?**  
A: Adults: $150 per year for lenses/frames or contact lenses. Dependents under 18: $100 per year. One comprehensive eye exam per year is covered at 100% with a network provider.

**Q: Is LASIK covered?**  
A: No. Laser vision correction (e.g., LASIK) is not covered under the base Global Health Plan.

### Mental Health and EAP

**Q: How many therapy sessions are covered?**  
A: Up to 20 outpatient mental health sessions per calendar year without prior authorization. Additional sessions may require prior authorization. EAP (Employee Assistance Program) sessions are separate and do not count toward the 20.

**Q: How do I access the EAP?**  
A: Contact information for the EAP is in the benefits portal and in materials from HR. The EAP provides short-term counseling and referrals for personal or work-related issues.

### Pharmacy and Prescriptions

**Q: Where can I fill prescriptions in Nigeria?**  
A: Use a network pharmacy. MedPlus, HealthPlus, May & Baker (designated branches), and other listed network pharmacies in Lagos and Ibadan are in the plan. The full HMO/provider list in Section 4 includes pharmacy networks.

**Q: What are the prescription copay tiers?**  
A: Tier 1 (generic) has the lowest copay; Tier 2 (preferred brand) and Tier 3 (non-preferred) have higher copays. Exact amounts are in the plan document or carrier summary.

### General

**Q: What if I relocate from Nigeria to the US (or vice versa)?**  
A: Update HR with your new work location. Your network (Nigeria vs US) and ID cards will be updated. Coverage continues under the same Global Health Plan.

**Q: Is there a separate card for dental and vision?**  
A: Depending on the administrator, you may receive one combined card or separate cards. Check the benefits portal or ask HR.

**Q: Who do I contact for questions?**  
A: For eligibility, enrollment, and general questions: HR (hr@vectixlogic.com). For claims and network: the plan administrator (contact in benefits portal). For emergencies, use the emergency number on your ID card.

## 8. Appendix: Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Global Health Plan (GHP)** | VectixLogic's primary medical, dental, vision, and mental health plan. |
| **HMO** | Health Maintenance Organization; network of providers and facilities. |
| **Premium** | Cost of insurance; company pays 100% employee, 50% up to two dependents. |
| **Dependent** | Spouse or child as defined in plan documents. |
| **Full-time** | Employment status eligible for GHP; see HR for definition. |
| **Network** | Facilities and providers that have agreed to plan rates; use for best coverage. |
| **Pre-authorization** | Approval required before certain procedures or admissions. |
| **SPD** | Summary Plan Description; US legal summary of plan terms. |
| **EAP** | Employee Assistance Program; short-term counseling and referrals. |
| **Copay** | Fixed amount you pay at time of service (e.g., pharmacy tier). |
| **Reasonable and customary** | Usual rate for a service in the region; reimbursement cap for out-of-network. |
| **Qualifying life event** | Marriage, birth, loss of other coverage, etc.; allows mid-year enrollment change. |
| **Open enrollment** | Annual period when you can change elections. |
| **Appeal** | Request for review of a denied claim; 180 days from denial. |
| **Tier 1–3** | Prescription drug tiers (generic, preferred brand, non-preferred). |
