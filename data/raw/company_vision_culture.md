## 1. Founders' Story

VectixLogic LLC was founded at the intersection of pharmacy practice and software engineering. The founders—a licensed pharmacist with experience in hospital and community practice in Nigeria, and a software engineer focused on secure, high-performance systems—saw a clear gap: pharmacy operations and health-tech in Nigeria and in cross-border contexts needed tools that were both compliant (PCN, NAFDAC, FDA/DSCSA) and built for scale. At the same time, the team had a shared interest in blockchain and decentralized systems, which led to work on Solana-based applications and, eventually, the "Vectix Foundry" platform for building and deploying secure agents and integrations.

The company name "VectixLogic" reflects a vector toward clarity and logic—bringing structure, traceability, and clear reasoning to complex domains: pharmacy supply chain, real estate (GIDA), and developer tooling. We started with a small team in Ibadan and Lagos and expanded to include a presence in Wyoming, USA, to better serve global clients and remote talent. Our culture is built on three pillars: **excellence in execution**, **compliance by design**, and **inclusive, remote-first collaboration**. We believe that great software can improve both patient outcomes and the day-to-day experience of pharmacists and developers, and we hold ourselves to high standards in security, testing, and documentation.

## 2. Mission and Values

- **Mission:** To build secure, compliant software that simplifies complex operations in pharmacy, proptech, and blockchain—enabling our clients and partners to focus on their core mission.
- **Values:** Integrity (we do not cut corners on compliance or security); clarity (we communicate in plain language and document decisions); and continuous improvement (we use TDD, code review, and post-incident reviews to get better).

## 3. 2030 Product Roadmap (Strategic Direction)

The following is a high-level product and market direction for VectixLogic through 2030. It is not a commitment to specific release dates but a statement of strategic intent.

### 3.1 Pharmacy and Health-Tech (2026–2028)

- **Unified pharmacy platform:** A single, scalable platform that supports inventory (including Schedule II and controlled substances), expiry management, Return for Credit (RFC), and full DSCSA/PCN/NAFDAC-aligned traceability. Target: Nigerian hospital and retail pharmacies, with optional modules for US dispensers.
- **Interoperability:** Deeper integration with HMOs, wholesalers, and manufacturers (e.g., electronic TI/TH/TS, NAFDAC verification, and recall management).
- **Analytics and reporting:** Dashboards for inventory turnover, expiry risk, and compliance readiness to support pharmacy managers and auditors.

### 3.2 Vectix Foundry and Developer Platform (2026–2029)

- **Agent framework:** Production-ready framework for building, deploying, and monitoring AI agents (e.g., customer support, internal knowledge RAG) with safety, auditability, and cost controls.
- **Blockchain tooling:** Solana SDK wrappers, transaction builders, and smart contract utilities used internally and offered to partners; emphasis on security (see VL-TECH-016) and gas efficiency.
- **API-first and ecosystem:** Public and partner APIs for pharmacy, GIDA (real estate), and identity/career (e.g., Vectix career-identity) with clear SLAs and documentation.

### 3.3 GIDA and Proptech (2027–2030)

- **Housing and land intelligence:** Expand GIDA-related agents and data products for the Nigerian housing market—bias-free recommendations, transparent pricing, and compliance with local regulations.
- **Partnerships:** Collaborate with developers, agencies, and government bodies to standardize data and improve access to housing information.

### 3.4 Organizational and Geographic (2026–2030)

- **Talent and culture:** Grow the engineering and pharmacy consulting teams while maintaining a remote-first, high-trust culture. Invest in learning (certifications, Master’s support per VL-BEN-004) and career pathways (VL-HR-015).
- **Sustainability and governance:** Formalize ESG and governance practices; ensure our products and operations align with our mission and with evolving regulatory expectations in each market.

## 4. How This Informs Day-to-Day Work

- **Engineers:** Roadmap priorities guide which systems we invest in (e.g., DSCSA, Foundry, GIDA). Every feature should align with compliance, security, and our technical standards (VL-TECH-016).
- **Pharmacy and operations:** Our pharmacy tools are not side projects—they are core to our identity and to the 2030 vision. Quality, auditability, and user safety come first.
- **Everyone:** Our founders’ story and 2030 roadmap are the "why" behind our policies (PTO, remote work, AI ethics, client communication). When in doubt, we choose the option that best serves long-term trust and compliance.
